Why Protest?

It is your right and responsibility as a taxpayer to make sure the appraisal district is taxing your home properly. No one should pay more than their fair share of taxes. It is very important to protest if you are nearing the age of 65 as you want to keep your value as low as possible. In addition, if you have condition issues with your home, the district may not be aware of those issues

2019 Time Line

JANUARY 1ST:

Appraisal District applies a market value as of January 1st on all properties in their county. What was your home or business worth on January 1st? Contact me and I will let you know my opinion.

JANUARY 31ST:

For most properties, this is the last day to pay the taxes to avoid penalties and interest. File for Homestead, OA, or Disabled Exemptions for up to 2 years prior.

April 1st:

Most Texas Appraisal Districts will begin mailing out Notices of Appraised Value. Property Owners/Agents will have 30 days from posted date or date printed on the Notice to file a protest for 2019. This is also the final deadline for filing Personal Property Renditions.

April 30th:

The final deadline to file for Agriculture special values, Historical Exemptions, and many other exemption forms are due.

May 1st:

On or around the 1st of May will be the deadline for appeal for most Texas Counties.

Hearings

Property Hearings Begin shortly after Property Notices are Mailed
